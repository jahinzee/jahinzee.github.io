# IREC: Software & Websites

## Software

Most of the software here are cross-platform on the desktop.

- [Boxy SVG](https://boxy-svg.com/)
  – a simple and easy-to-use SVG editor.

- [Deskflow](https://deskflow.org/)[^os]
  – share one seat (mouse and keyboard) across multiple computers - like a KVM,
    but in software!

- [KeePassXC](https://keepassxc.org/)[^os][^lf]
  – self-hosted encrypted password manager.

- [LyX](https://www.lyx.org/)[^os]
  – LaTeX meets traditional word processor workflows.

- [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/)[^lf]
  – a super-flexible, Local-First, markdown based notetaking and writing app.

- [Paint.NET](https://getpaint.net/)
  – a powerful yet simple painting and image manipulation tool. (Windows only)

- [Qalculate](https://qalculate.github.io/)[^os]
  – a powerful multi-purpose calculator.

- [Syncthing](https://syncthing.net/)[^os][^lf]
  – sync files between devices like it's Y2K! LAN-oriented, peer-to-peer, and
  secure — no cloud required.

- [Thunderbird](https://www.thunderbird.net)[^os]
  – no-nonsense, flexible and powerful email client.

- [VSCodium](https://vscodium.com/)[^os]
  – fully open-source distribution of Visual Studio Code—sans Microsoft.

## Websites

<Infocard logo="information-2">

While I have made every effort to ensure that these websites are safe and not overtly annoying to
use at the time of inclusion, links and resources may change at any time. Visit at your own risk!

</Infocard>

- [cobalt](https://cobalt.tools/)
  – online media downloader, without the dodginess.

- [CyberChef](https://gchq.github.io/CyberChef/)
  – various data processing tools for programmers.

- [Desmos](https://www.desmos.com/calculator)
  – graphing calculator.

- [Excalidraw](https://excalidraw.com/)
  – web-based whiteboard - great for flowcharts and diagrams. All data is
    stored locally, and it can run offline as a progressive web app.

- [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/)
  – need I say more?

- [Jisho](https://jisho.org/)
  – a clean and powerful Japanese-English dictionary.

- [Lexilogos - Multilingual Keyboard](https://www.lexilogos.com/keyboard/index.htm)
  – type in almost any script imaginable - all you need is a standard Latin
    keyboard.

- [Pexels](https://www.pexels.com/) and [Unsplash](https://unsplash.com/)
  – stock photos, also a great source of wallpapers.

- [RapidTables](https://www.rapidtables.com/)
  – a collections of various converters, calculators and other utilities.

- [Regex101](https://regex101.com/)
  – build, test and debug regular expressions.

- [Remix Icon](https://remixicon.com/)
  – open-source, expansive and clean icon set.

- [Wikimedia Commons](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Main_Page)
  – massive library of free[^1] media.

- [Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Wiktionary:Main_Page)
  – free[^1] dictionary, thesaurus, abbreviation reference, and more - fit
    for all your literary needs.

[^os]: Open source, as defined by the
       [Open Source Initiative](https://opensource.org/definition-annotated).
[^lf]: Local-First, as defined by
       [Ink and Switch](https://www.inkandswitch.com/local-first/).
[^1]: "free" as in "free speech"
